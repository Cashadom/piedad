A Contemporary Sacred Vision

This powerful acrylic painting reimagines the iconic "Pietà" sculpture by Tommaso Bandini (1807-1849) from the Sanctuary of Santa Maria della Steccata in Parma, Italy. Through vibrant colors and expressive brushwork, Eduardo Jiménez Aguirre brings new life to this sacred moment between the Virgin Mary and Christ, bridging centuries of artistic tradition with contemporary vision.

About the Artist

Eduardo Jiménez Aguirre is a contemporary Mexican artist whose work explores religious themes through modern techniques and vibrant expression. His interpretation of "La Piedad" demonstrates his exceptional ability to bridge classical sacred art with contemporary vision.

Working primarily in acrylic, Jiménez Aguirre creates works that honor traditional religious iconography while bringing fresh perspective through bold color palettes and dynamic brushwork that speaks to today's collectors and art enthusiasts.

The Sacred Tradition

This painting draws inspiration from the 19th-century marble sculpture by Tommaso Bandini, housed in the Sanctuary of Santa Maria della Steccata in Parma, Italy. This lesser-known "Pietà" continues the artistic tradition established by Michelangelo's renowned Vatican masterpiece.

Jiménez Aguirre's interpretation translates the cold beauty of marble into vibrant acrylic tones, maintaining the profound emotional intensity while adding new dimensions through contemporary painterly technique. The square format creates a balanced, intimate composition that draws viewers into this timeless moment of divine sorrow and grace.
